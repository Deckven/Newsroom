## 1. HIGHSEC (Security 1.0–0.5)

### CONCORD Mechanics
- CONCORD is an invincible NPC police force that responds to criminal aggression in highsec
- **Response times by security level** (when CONCORD has just spawned in system):
  - 1.0–0.9: ~6 seconds
  - 0.8: ~7 seconds
  - 0.7: ~10 seconds
  - 0.6: ~14 seconds
  - 0.5: ~19 seconds
- If CONCORD is already on grid: 2–8 seconds regardless of sec status
- If CONCORD is elsewhere in system: 19–24 seconds
- CONCORD response is **unavoidable** — it cannot be tanked, evaded, or destroyed
- Two CONCORD Police Captains arrive first (e-war, cap drain, ~1,000 damage/volley), followed 15 seconds later by a CONCORD Police Commander (one-shot destruction)

### Criminal/Suspect Flags
- Attacking an innocent player = Criminal flag (CONCORD response + 15-minute criminal timer)
- Suspect flag (e.g., looting yellow wrecks) = anyone can attack you without CONCORD response
- Security status loss for destroying ships/pods

### Gate/Station Sentry Guns (also in lowsec)
- Damage: 80 EM + 140 Thermal + 80 Kinetic + 140 Explosive = 440 per volley
- DPS: ~176 damage/second (2.5s cycle)
- Range: 500km optimal + 50km falloff
- Tracking: 160 rad/s (hits everything)
- Invulnerable — cannot be targeted or destroyed
- Aggro resets when leaving grid

### Restrictions
- No capital ships (Capital Ships skill required vessels cannot enter)
- No bombs (from stealth bombers)
- No warp disruption bubbles
- No cynosural fields / jump beacons
- No anchoring containers in 0.9–1.0 space

### Wardec (War Declaration) Mechanics
- **Cost**: Flat fee of 100 million ISK per week
- **Eligibility**: Only corps/alliances owning an Upwell structure or customs office can declare or receive wars
- NPC corp members are immune to wardecs
- **Timeline**: 24-hour startup → 1 week active combat → 24-hour cooldown
- **Ending wars**: Non-renewal, surrender (prevents re-declaration for 2 weeks), destruction of attacker's war HQ, retraction
- **Mutual wars**: No weekly fee, never expire without manual action, all allies removed
- **Ally system**: Defenders can recruit allies (4-hour prep after acceptance)

### Advantages
- Safest space — CONCORD protection
- Close to trade hubs, easy logistics
- Low barrier to entry for new players
- No risk of bubble camps or gatecamps (bubbles prohibited)
- Incursions provide top-tier fleet income
- Abyssal Deadspace accessible from any highsec station

### Disadvantages
- Lowest passive income potential (worst ore, lowest bounties)
- Wardecs can make life dangerous for structure-owning corps
- Suicide ganking still possible (especially on trade routes)
- Overcrowded anomalies and mining belts
- No sovereignty mechanics, limited territorial gameplay
- Mission rewards and LP are lower than in lowsec/nullsec

## 2. LOWSEC (Security 0.4–0.1)

### Security Mechanics
- **No CONCORD** — police do not respond to aggression
- Gate/station sentry guns still present (fewer than highsec): 440 dmg/volley, 176 DPS, 500km range
- Aggression causes security status loss
- Sentry aggro resets on leaving grid (warp off and back = fresh start)
- NPC faction navy patrols attack low-standing players

### Faction Warfare (FW)
- **Four empire factions**: Amarr vs Minmatar, Caldari vs Gallente
- **Two pirate factions**: Angel Cartel (Malakim Zealots), Guristas (Commando Guri)
- **Joining requirements**: 0.0+ standing toward chosen faction (solo), or corp/alliance enrollment
- **War zones**:
  - Amarr-Minmatar: Devoid, Bleak Lands, Heimatar, Metropolis (70 systems)
  - Caldari-Gallente: Black Rise, Citadel, Essence, Verge Vendor, Placid (101 systems)

#### Plex Mechanics
- Complex types by size and capture time:
  - Scout: 10 min (frigates only)
  - Small: 10 min (destroyers and below)
  - Medium: 15 min (cruisers and below)
  - Large: 15 min (battleships and below)
  - Open: 15 min (unrestricted)
- Capture requires staying within 30km of point; timer pauses if enemy enters
- System vulnerability at 75,000 victory points

#### LP Rewards (Frontline systems get +50%)
- Scout: 10,000–15,000 LP
- Small: 15,000–22,500 LP
- Medium: 20,000–30,000 LP
- Large: 25,000–37,500 LP
- Open: 30,000–45,000 LP
- Battlefield: 150,000 LP (up to 30 players)
- Ship kills: 3.25 x (hull value - insurance + modules) / 10,000, capped at 50,000 LP

#### Tier System (based on system control %)
- Tier 1 (0–20%): -50% LP
- Tier 2 (21–40%): base
- Tier 3 (41–60%): +75% LP
- Tier 4 (61–80%): +150% LP
- Tier 5 (81–100%): +225% LP

#### Insurgency Mechanics
- Pirate militias can increase Corruption in systems
- At Level 5 Corruption: bombs and bubbles permitted in FW systems
- Empire militias increase Suppression to counter pirate influence

### DED Complexes
- Ratings 1/10 through 10/10 (no 9/10 exists)
- **Highsec**: 1/10–5/10
- **Lowsec**: 1/10–8/10
- **Nullsec**: 1/10–10/10
- Ship restrictions: 1/10 = frigates only, scaling up to 5/10+ = battleships
- Loot: deadspace modules, pirate ship BPCs, faction modules (0–5 drops per overseer)
- Lowsec 5/10–6/10 sites can drop modules worth 100M–2B+ ISK

### Moon Mining
- Available in 0.5 and below
- Requires Athanor or Tatara refinery with Standup Moon Drill
- Moon goo is essential for T2 production
- Can be very profitable but requires infrastructure investment

### PvP Culture
- Small gang PvP is the dominant playstyle
- Faction Warfare provides structured PvP content with rewards
- Gate camps on lowsec entry systems are common
- Pirate groups and solo PvPers frequent lowsec
- FW plexes create natural "arenas" with ship class restrictions

### Advantages
- Higher LP rewards (80% more than highsec agents)
- Access to moon mining
- DED 5/10–8/10 sites (lucrative)
- Active small-gang PvP scene
- FW provides structured content with good rewards
- Lower population density than highsec
- No wardec required to fight (but security status matters)

### Disadvantages
- Constant PvP threat without CONCORD backup
- Gate camps at highsec-lowsec borders
- Security status loss from aggression (can be recovered via tags)
- Sentry guns punish aggression near gates/stations
- Limited infrastructure compared to nullsec
- Need to manage security status carefully
- Less organized logistics than null blocs

## 3. NULLSEC (Security 0.0 and below)

### Two Types

#### Sovereign (Claimable) Nullsec
- 30+ regions where player alliances claim sovereignty
- Player-owned stations (Keepstars, Fortizars, etc.) — often only allies can dock
- Full sovereignty mechanics with Sovereignty Hubs (post-Equinox expansion, June 2024)

#### NPC Nullsec
- Six major regions: **Curse, Great Wildlands, Outer Ring, Stain, Syndicate, Venal**
- Plus individual NPC constellations in sov regions
- Cannot be claimed by players
- NPC stations always available for docking
- Popular for independent groups, nomadic playstyles

### Core Mechanics
- **No CONCORD, no gate guns, no sentry guns**
- PvP aggression causes zero security status loss
- Bombs from stealth bombers allowed
- Warp disruption bubbles allowed (interdictors, heavy interdictors, mobile deployables)
- Cynosural fields functional (for capital ship movement)
- Full lawlessness — anything goes

### Sovereignty Mechanics (Post-Equinox 2024)

#### Sovereignty Hub (replaced TCU/IHub)
- Central structure for system control
- Requires resources: **Power** (from star), **Workforce** (from planets), **Reagents** (superionic ice + magmatic gas)

#### Activity Defense Multiplier (ADM)
- Three indices: Military, Industrial, Strategic (each level 0–5)
- Military/Industrial: +0.6/+1.2/+1.7/+2.1/+2.5 per level
- Strategic: +0.4/+0.6/+0.8/+0.9/+1.0 per level
- Maximum ADM: 6x (with capital system +2 bonus)
- **Vulnerability window**: 18 hours / ADM (at ADM 6 = 3 hours)
- **Reinforcement timer**: Base 10 min x ADM (at ADM 6 = 60 min)

#### Infrastructure Upgrades
- **Strategic**: Ansiblex Jump Bridges (Strat Index 3), Cyno Beacons (Strat 2), Cyno Jammers (Strat 1), Supercapital Shipyards
- **Industrial**: Mineral Prospecting Arrays (7 types, 3 tiers) — spawn guaranteed ore sites
- **Military**: Threat Detection Arrays — spawn combat anomalies (havens, sanctums, etc.)

#### Capture Process
1. Attacker uses Entosis Link during vulnerability window
2. Structure enters reinforcement (timer based on ADM)
3. Command nodes spawn across constellation
4. Attackers need 9+ node captures (7% progress each); defenders need 6+

### ESS (Encounter Surveillance System)
- Present in **every sovereign nullsec system**
- **Main Bank**: Holds 40% of all NPC bounties in system
  - Pays out every 2 hours 45 minutes if undisturbed
  - Theft: sit within 10km, link (6-minute countdown), receive ~80% in tags
  - Tags exchangeable at CONCORD stations
- **Reserve Bank**: Larger accumulation over time
  - Requires ESS Reserve Security Keys (8 types)
  - Keys found in SCC Secure Key Storage data sites in **lowsec**
  - Theft triggers regional alert (not just system)
- **Field restrictions**: Tactical destroyers through battleships only (no Marauders)
  - No cloaking within 500km
  - 75km interdiction bubble
  - No mobile deployables within 1,000km

### Ratting (NPC Bounties)
- Primary anomaly types (increasing difficulty/reward):
  - Forsaken Rally Points → Forsaken Hubs → Havens → Sanctums
- Bounties paid every 20 minutes (not instant)
- 40% held in ESS main bank (effectively a bounty tax)
- Anomalies can escalate to DED-rated combat sites (10/10s in null = very lucrative)
- Capital spawns: 60–240M ISK bounties

### Mining in Nullsec
- **Ore types**: Bistot, Arkonor, Spodumain, Mercoxit (null-exclusive ores), plus Kernite, Pyroxeres
- **Rorqual**: Capital industrial ship, ~89 days training
  - 300,000 m3 mining hold
  - 1,000,000 m3 ship maintenance bay
  - 10% drone mining yield per level
  - Capital compressors for ore compression
  - PANIC module (invulnerability for limited time when under attack)
  - Cannot enter highsec
- **Moon mining**: Full R4–R64 moon goo available
- Mineral Prospecting Arrays from Sov Hub spawn guaranteed ore sites

### Major Alliances & Politics (as of 2025–2026)
- **Goonswarm Federation** (Imperium) — Delve/Querious/Fountain
- **Pandemic Horde** (PanFam) — various regions
- **Fraternity** (FRT/WinterCo) — Drone regions, Vale of the Silent
- **TEST Alliance Please Ignore** — various
- **Brave Collective** — newbie-friendly null alliance
- **Pandemic Legion** — elite PvP
- "Blue donut" politics: large coalitions with mutual non-aggression pacts reduce PvP opportunities in sov space
- Major wars can involve thousands of players (B-R5RB, M2-XFE scale battles)

### Requirements to Join Major Alliances
- Most accept new players (especially Brave, KarmaFleet/Goons, Pandemic Horde)
- Typical requirements: working mic, willingness to fleet up, minimum 5–10M SP for some corps
- Elite corps: 30–50M+ SP, killboard review, interview, vouch system
- ESI (EVE Swagger Interface) API check standard for security screening
- Must use alliance comms (Discord/Mumble/TeamSpeak)

### Income Sources & Estimated ISK/Hour

| Activity | ISK/Hour (approx.) | Requirements |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Subcap ratting (Ishtar/Gila) | 30–60M (after ESS) | ~20M SP, cruiser skills |
| Carrier ratting (Havens) | 60–120M (after ESS) | ~40M SP, carrier skills |
| Supercarrier ratting | 100–200M (after ESS) | ~60M SP, super + support |
| Marauder ratting | 80–150M | ~30M SP, marauder skills |

### Advantages
- Highest bounty income from ratting
- Best ore in the game (null-exclusive minerals)
- Full infrastructure: jump bridges, cyno beacons, jammers
- Alliance provides defense fleet, logistics, buyback programs
- Sovereignty gives territorial control and pride
- Ship Replacement Programs (SRP) for PvP losses
- Large-scale fleet warfare content

### Disadvantages
- Bubble camps — interdiction bubbles on gates catch anyone
- Intelligence required (watching intel channels, local)
- Blue donut can make space boring (no nearby enemies)
- ESS tax on bounties (40% held, theft risk)
- CTA (Call to Arms) obligations — must defend space
- Long logistics chains to trade hubs
- Capital ship investment required for top-tier income
- Risk of losing sovereignty in wars

## 4. WORMHOLE SPACE (J-Space)

### Overview
- ~2,600 known systems + 100 shattered wormhole systems
- All system names: "J" + 6 digits (e.g., J123456)
- **No local chat** — players only appear in local when they type
- **No stargates** — only wormhole connections
- **No NPC stations** — players live out of citadels (Astrahus, Fortizar, Keepstar) or POSes
- **No sovereignty mechanics** — territorial control is informal
- Supercapitals cannot enter wormhole space

### Wormhole Classes

|-------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| C1 | 348 | 62,000,000 kg (no battleships) | Beginner-friendly, no escalations |
| C2 | 525 | 300,000,000 kg | Dual statics (K-space + W-space), best logistics |

| C4 | 505 | 300,000,000 kg | Dual W-space statics, no direct K-space |
| C5 | 512 | 1,350,000,000 kg | "C5 Highway", capitals allowed, escalations |
| C6 | 113 | 1,350,000,000 kg | Hardest content, capital escalations, highest ISK |

### Static Wormholes
- Each WH system has 1–2 "static" connections that always lead to the same class
- When a static collapses, a new one spawns to a different system of same class
- C2s are prized: one static to K-space (easy logistics) + one to W-space (content)
- C4s have two W-space statics but no guaranteed K-space exit

### Wormhole Mass & Lifetime
- **Per-transit limit**: Maximum mass for a single jump (e.g., B274 = 375,000,000 kg)
- **Total mass**: Cumulative before collapse (e.g., B274 = 2,000,000,000 kg ± 10%)
- **Lifetime indicators**:
  - "Not yet begun to decay" = >24 hours
  - "Beginning to decay" = <24 hours
  - "Reaching end of life" = <4 hours
  - "On the verge of collapse" = <1 hour (some sources say <15 min)
- **Mass indicators**: "Has not yet had its stability disrupted" → "Has had its stability reduced but not critical" → "Verge of collapse"

### PvE Content

#### Sleeper Combat Sites
- Sleepers are advanced AI NPCs: switch targets, use e-war, remote repair
- Drop "blue loot" (NPC buy orders) + salvage for T3 production materials
- **No bounties** — all income from loot and salvage
- Capital escalations in C5/C6: additional battleship waves triggered by capitals on grid

#### Gas Huffing
- Fullerene gas (exclusive to wormholes) — essential for T3 production
- C320 and C540 fullerenes (C5/C6 only) are most valuable
- Venture is standard ship (2x Gas Cloud Scoop I = 40 units/minute)
- Gas Cloud Harvesting skill required (skillbook ~30M ISK)
- Gas sites are lightly guarded (Sleeper NPCs spawn after ~15-20 minutes)
- "Ninja huffing" = huff gas before NPCs spawn, then leave

#### Data/Relic Sites
- Pirate sites in C1–C3 (unguarded, same as null pirate sites)
- Sleeper data/relic sites (heavily guarded, require combat fleet)

#### Ore Mining
- Arkonor, Bistot, Gneiss, Kernite, Omber, Pyroxeres available
- Higher classes have more abundant ore sites

#### Planetary Industry
- WH PI planets are often rich and uncrowded
- Tax depends on POCO (Player-Owned Customs Office) owner settings
- Excellent passive income supplement

### Scanning Lifestyle
- Probe scanning is essential — must scan every connection
- Core Scanner Probes for signatures, Combat Scanner Probes for ships
- Astrometrics + support skills critical
- Bookmark management is vital (label all connections with destination, mass state, lifetime)
- Third-party tools: Pathfinder, Tripwire, Siggy for mapping chains

### No Local Chat — Implications
- Cannot see who is in system without D-scan or combat probes
- Cloaky ships are invisible until they decloak
- Creates constant paranoia/awareness requirement
- D-scan must be used frequently (every few seconds during PvE)
- Combat probes on D-scan = immediate threat

### Special Systems
- **Thera**: Named system with 4 Sisters of EVE NPC stations, multiple connections (tracked by EVE-Scout)
- **Drifter Wormholes**: Hub systems with 50–70 connections, Drifter battleship guards, C2-strength effects
- **Shattered Wormholes**: 100 systems, shattered planets (no moons = no moon mining, no POCOs for PI)
- **C13 (Small-ship shattered)**: Frigate-class wormholes, Wolf-Rayet effect (+200% small weapon damage)

### Major WH Groups (as of 2025–2026)
- **Hard Knocks Inc. [HKRAB]** — historically dominant C6/C5 group
- **Lazerhawks [LZHX]** — elite C5/C6 PvP
- **Hole Control** — C5 space
- **No Vacancies [NVACA]** — established WH alliance
- **Wormhole Society** — community group
- **Wingspan Delivery Services** — torpedo delivery (ganking explorers/ratters)
- Many smaller corps in C2–C4 space

### Advantages
- Highest potential ISK/hour (C5/C6 capital escalations)
- No local = excellent for ambush PvP
- Self-sufficient community feeling
- Rich PI planets
- Exclusive T3 production materials
- Less "blue donut" politics
- Mass limits create natural fight size limits (no N+1 blobbing)
- Gas huffing accessible to new players (Venture + basic skills)

### Disadvantages
- Must scan constantly — no fixed routes
- Logistics nightmare (hauling through wormhole chains)
- Citadel is your only home — if destroyed, you lose everything inside
- No asset safety (unlike K-space structures)
- No local chat = constant paranoia
- Evictions are devastating (organized groups destroy your citadel)
- Isolation from trade hubs
- Steep learning curve for scanning, bookmarking, hole control
- C5/C6 requires capital ships = massive investment
- If your connection collapses while you're out, you need to scan your way home

## 6. ABYSSAL DEADSPACE (Cross-Space Activity)

### Mechanics
- Entered via filaments from anywhere (including highsec station undock)
- **20-minute hard timer** — ship and pod destroyed if exceeded
- Three rooms connected by gates, ~6.5 min per room average
- Upon exit: appear at filament activation point (PvP vulnerability)

### Tiers
- T0 (Tranquil) through T6 (Cataclysmic)
- T0–T3: -30% or -50% random penalties
- T4–T6: -50% or -70% penalties

### Ship Options
- 1 Cruiser (T1/T2/Navy/Pirate, no T3 Strategic Cruisers)
- 2 Destroyers (costs 2 filaments; T3 tactical destroyers allowed)
- 3 Frigates (costs 3 filaments)

### Weather Types
- **Dark**: -turret range, +velocity
- **Electrical**: -EM resist, -cap recharge time (faster)
- **Exotic**: -kinetic resist, +scan resolution
- **Firestorm**: -thermal resist, +armor HP
- **Gamma**: -explosive resist, +shield HP

### Loot
- Mutaplasmids (weather-dependent)
- Triglavian blueprints and skill books
- Production materials (Crystalline Isogen-10, Zero-Point Condensate)
- Higher-tier filaments

## Key Takeaways for Article

1. **New players**: Start in highsec, try FW in lowsec early (low ship cost, good LP)
2. **Solo players**: Highsec (missions/abyssals), lowsec FW, C3 wormholes
3. **Social players**: Nullsec alliances (sov experience), wormhole corps (tight-knit)
4. **ISK seekers**: C5/C6 wormholes (highest ceiling), Incursions (consistent highsec), Nullsec ratting (steady with alliance support)
5. **PvP seekers**: Lowsec FW (accessible), Wormholes (skilled small gang), Nullsec (fleet warfare)
6. **Risk-averse**: Highsec always, with Abyssals/Incursions for high income
7. **Self-sufficient**: Wormholes reward preparation and adaptability
