### Fanfest Guest Speaker Mark Crowther
**Date:** March 5, 2026
**Source:** [eveonline.com](https://www.eveonline.com/news/view/fanfest-guest-speaker-mark-crowther)

CCP Games has announced Professor Mark Crowther as the third guest speaker for EVE Fanfest 2026 in Reykjavik. Known in-game as Crowthrm Kaundur, Crowther is a professor and chair of medicine at McMaster University, where he holds the Leo Pharma Chair in Thrombosis Research. A longtime EVE University member, he is also a contributor to the Blood Tome: Poetic EVE project, which documents New Eden's history through verse. His keynote will bridge his dual expertise in hematology and EVE lore, exploring how blood functions as both a biological mechanism and a narrative symbol within the game. Topics will include the science of blood production, Blood Raider mythology, the Crimson Harvest storyline, and thematic elements of immortality and identity. Fanfest ticket availability is limited, and CCP encourages prospective attendees to register promptly.

### The Capsuleer Edda — Latest News
**Date:** March 3, 2026
**Source:** [eveonline.com](https://www.eveonline.com/news/view/the-capsuleer-edda-latest-news)

The Capsuleer Edda is an ambitious cultural preservation project aiming to translate EVE Online's player-driven history into a physical manuscript designed to endure for millennia. The tome will be hand-written on vellum using a 17th-century ink formulation combined with blood voluntarily donated by Fanfest attendees. Each participant contributes a single drop, pooled into a composite material — a symbolic gesture binding capsuleers to the physical record. The process is non-clinical, voluntary, anonymous, and overseen by trained professionals, with registration closing April 2 at 15:00 UTC. Icelandic authors Andri Snaer Magnason and Jonas Reynir Gunnarsson are composing epic poetry reflecting EVE's emergent history, which a professional calligrapher will inscribe by hand. The manuscript will be left unsigned, honoring the tradition of Iceland's medieval sagas. The project addresses a fundamental question: how can digital cultures, often ephemeral due to corporate ownership, be preserved in durable physical form?

### EVE Evolved: Sharper Skies
**Date:** February 24, 2026
**Source:** [eveonline.com](https://www.eveonline.com/news/view/eve-evolved-sharper-skies)

CCP's art team has delivered a complete visual overhaul of asteroids in the Version 23.02 update, targeting higher fidelity, model variation across sizes, and greater texture diversity between ore types. The original asteroids used identical models regardless of scale, creating an unrealistic appearance. The team sculpted entirely new high-polygon rock, crystal, and ice variants, expanding from 4 to 12 variations per type with distinct small, medium, and large versions. Crystal proportions now scale realistically — smaller rocks show proportionally larger crystals, while larger specimens feature increased crystal density. Ice asteroids were rebuilt from scratch with designs inspired by classic iceberg and mountain-ridge formations. The technical pipeline used Houdini 3D software to convert high-poly source models into optimized game-ready assets, baking normal maps, occlusion data, and surface information while minimizing texture memory usage. Six ORE ships — Endurance, Noctis, Porpoise, Primae, Prospect, and Venture — also received lighting and VFX modernization. CCP emphasized these changes are purely visual with no gameplay impact.

### EVE Evolved: Aura Guidance
**Date:** February 17, 2026
**Source:** [eveonline.com](https://www.eveonline.com/news/view/eve-evolved-aura-guidance)

CCP has introduced Aura Guidance, an AI-powered contextual help system built to reduce new player confusion without requiring them to leave the game client. The system was constructed by analyzing 5.8 million messages from the in-game Rookie Help channel collected between January and September 2025, from which 706,000 genuine questions were extracted and processed into vetted Q&A pairs with player names and sensitive information removed. When a new player asks a question, the system embeds it, matches against the Q&A bank, and synthesizes a response enriched with the player's context — their location, ship type, and recent activity. Critically, the architecture prioritizes safety over capability: all responses come from vetted pairs only, with no open-ended generation. When confidence is low, players are redirected to Rookie Help instead of receiving a guess. CCP's team, drawing on healthcare AI experience, designed the system to avoid hallucinations. It is currently in controlled A/B testing with new characters, measuring success solely on player retention. CCP stated that failure is an acceptable outcome if the feature does not demonstrably improve results.

### EVE Creator Awards
**Date:** February 12, 2026
**Source:** [eveonline.com](https://www.eveonline.com/news/view/eve-creator-awards)

CCP has announced the inaugural EVE Creator Awards, a formal recognition program for community creators with a ceremony at Fanfest 2026 on May 16 at the Harpa concert hall in Reykjavik. The program establishes a new annual tradition aimed at formally acknowledging the diverse creators who shape the EVE Online community.

### The Ancient Gaming Noob (TAGN)

**New Eden Banter #1 — Top Ten Reasons EVE Online is Still a Thing in 2026**
**Date:** March 2, 2026
**Source:** [tagn.wordpress.com](https://tagn.wordpress.com/2026/03/02/new-eden-banter-1-top-ten-reasons-eve-online-is-still-a-thing-in-2026/)

Veteran blogger Wilhelm Arcturus responds to a blog banter prompt exploring EVE Online's remarkable longevity as the game approaches its 23rd year. His ten reasons paint a nuanced picture: sunk cost fallacy keeps veterans invested through accumulated skills and assets; the game's visual presentation — nebulae, planetary scales — still inspires genuine awe; dense lore exists without forcing engagement, letting players write their own narratives. EVE accommodates every MMO playstyle from solo missions to empire-building while also enabling activities no competitor matches, like space trucking and market manipulation. Shared frustration with CCP's decisions paradoxically unites the community. The crafting economy is meaningful because 6 million ships were destroyed in 2025 alone, creating perpetual demand. Unpredictable encounters still raise pulses — even routine hauling carries genuine adrenaline. PvP's thrill-and-agony dynamic creates unforgettable moments, though it limits mass appeal. Finally, player-driven history produces unrepeatable events like the legendary B-R5RB battle. Arcturus argues that EVE's chaos and unpredictability are both its greatest strength and its barrier to wider adoption.
