# World of Warcraft: Midnight - News Roundup (March 5, 2026)

## Article 1: WoW Midnight Goes Live March 2 (Blizzard Official)

**Source:** [Blizzard News](https://news.blizzard.com/en-us/article/24243639/world-of-warcraft-midnighttm-goes-live-march-2)

World of Warcraft: Midnight officially launched worldwide on March 2, 2026, at 3:00 PM PST, marking the second chapter of the Worldsoul Saga trilogy. The expansion raises the level cap to 90 and introduces four new zones: a reimagined Eversong Woods, the troll capital of Zul'Aman, the fungal jungle of Harandar, and the void-corrupted Voidstorm. Players can explore eight new dungeons and three raid tiers featuring nine bosses across The Voidspire, The Dreamrift, and March on Quel'Danas. The Haranir allied race debuts as a bioluminescent faction playable across nine classes for both Horde and Alliance. Demon Hunters gain a third specialization called Devourer, a void-themed mid-range DPS spec. Player housing arrives as a major new feature with modular layouts and community neighborhoods. Silvermoon City has been rebuilt as a shared faction hub. Additional features include the Prey hunting system, a new Slayer's Rise battleground, PvP Training Grounds for newcomers, transmog outfit saving, and significant UI enhancements. Three purchase editions offer varying cosmetic bundles, mounts, and early access perks.

## Article 2: Midnight Now Live Worldwide (Wowhead)

**Source:** [Wowhead](https://www.wowhead.com/news/world-of-warcraft-midnight-now-live-worldwide-380603)

Wowhead confirmed that WoW Midnight is now live for all players following the end of the early access period. The expansion launched on a Monday night followed by a standard weekly reset. While the Haranir allied race became immediately available, several major features unlocked after the weekly reset on March 3, including profession weekly quests, Arcantina visitor quests, Prey hard difficulty, and Mythic 0 dungeons with weekly lockouts (switching to daily lockouts on March 24). Season 1 competitive play is scheduled to begin on March 17. The expansion introduces four new zones with exploration content including rare spawns and hidden treasures. Wowhead has published comprehensive guides covering Silvermoon hub zones, world events, and rare locations across all new areas. Community reception has been mixed, with early reports highlighting significant server stability issues including crashes across multiple regions during the launch window. Players are settling into the new content while Blizzard addresses ongoing technical problems.

## Article 3: Class Tuning, Updates, and What to Expect (Blizzard Official)

**Source:** [Blizzard News](https://news.blizzard.com/en-us/article/24243864/class-tuning-updates-and-what-to-expect-in-midnight)

Blizzard outlined a structured approach to class balance for the Midnight expansion, acknowledging that "many specializations have undergone large changes." The tuning schedule follows key milestones: Season 1 begins March 17 with an initial tuning pass, Mythic raid and Mythic+ dungeons launch March 24 with follow-up adjustments, the March on Quel'Danas event goes live March 31, and a post-event worldwide tuning pass arrives April 7. Blizzard plans to perform additional tuning passes based on data gathered during the opening weeks and will be cautious during early progression to avoid disrupting competitive play. Monthly check-ins will follow as players acquire better gear and optimize builds. The studio emphasized these dates may shift as needed and reserved the right to deploy emergency hotfixes for significant bugs or balance issues outside the scheduled windows. This measured approach signals Blizzard's intent to let the meta develop naturally before making sweeping changes, giving players time to explore the new Apex Talents, rebuilt talent trees, and the Devourer specialization.

## Article 4: Hotfixes - March 4, 2026 (Blizzard Official)

**Source:** [Blizzard News](https://news.blizzard.com/en-us/article/24266320/hotfixes-march-4-2026)

Blizzard deployed a massive hotfix patch on March 4, addressing dozens of issues across nearly every game system. Class fixes were extensive: Frost Death Knights no longer dismount from Remorseless Winter damage, Devourer Demon Hunters received fixes for Eradicate and Void Metamorphosis bugs, and Feral/Guardian/Restoration Druids got corrections for Nurturing Instinct interactions. Hunters saw pathing improvements for Pack Leader's Boar and Tracker's Net crowd control adjustments. Mages received the most fixes, including corrections for Reverberate resource generation, Comet Storm usability, Scorch damage calculations, and multiple AoE targeting issues. Enhancement Shamans got critical fixes for Windfury Weapon dual-cast and Arc Discharge displaying wrong values (20% instead of intended 40%). On the systems side, rare profession tools switched from Bind-on-Pickup to Bind-on-Equip, Amani Extract Potion healing doubled, housing decor placement was expanded, and a Renown progression blocker beyond rank 11 was resolved. Multiple quest fixes addressed progression blockers in Voidstorm, Eversong Woods, and Quel'Danas content.

## Article 5: Midnight Early Access Patch Notes (Wowhead)

**Source:** [Wowhead](https://www.wowhead.com/news/official-midnight-content-update-patch-notes-for-early-access-380559)

The official Midnight patch notes revealed the full scope of the expansion's changes. All classes received major redesigns with rebuilt talent trees offering fewer mandatory filler points and new Apex Talent capstone abilities unlocking at level 81. The Devourer Demon Hunter specialization uses void power instead of fel, combining glaive combat with mid-range spellcasting, and Void Elves gain demon hunter access. The Prey system introduces trackable targets across three difficulty tiers offering exclusive rewards. PvP receives a Training Grounds mode where new players fight AI opponents, plus Mythic+ gets the new Lindormi's Guidance affix (levels 2-5) providing marked enemies and preventing timer deaths. Profession qualities have been reduced from three to two, streamlining the crafting system. The Catalyst system now unlocks charges after acquiring class set bonuses. Quality-of-life improvements include built-in damage meters, enhanced raid frames, and transmog sets saveable to action bars. Housing supports building, decorating, and personalizing homes through neighborhoods and endeavors. Eight new dungeons debut with four becoming Season 1 staples alongside returning legacy content.

## Article 6: Midnight Launch Maintenance Report (Blizzard Watch)

**Source:** [Blizzard Watch](https://blizzardwatch.com/2026/03/03/midnight-arrives-surprisingly-short-scheduled-wow-maintenance/)

Blizzard Watch reported on the scheduled server maintenance that accompanied Midnight's launch on March 3. The originally planned one-hour downtime was extended to two hours, running from 7-9 AM PT for Midnight realms. Mists of Pandaria Classic servers experienced longer downtime from 7-10 AM PT, while WoW Classic variants and Burning Crusade Anniversary servers followed the standard two-hour window. All WoW servers were confirmed back online after maintenance completion. The early access period, which began February 27, saw authentication server issues causing failed or slow login attempts, though these were resolved relatively quickly. The full launch on March 2 also triggered server crashes, but the extended maintenance on March 3 stabilized the infrastructure. Despite initial hiccups, Blizzard Watch described the maintenance as "surprisingly short" given the scope of the expansion. Servers have since been running smoothly, and players are diving into the new Quel'Thalas content, housing systems, and the Haranir allied race storyline.

## Article 7: Midnight Expansion Guide (Icy Veins)

**Source:** [Icy Veins](https://www.icy-veins.com/wow/midnight-expansion-guide)

Icy Veins published a comprehensive expansion guide covering all major Midnight features. The level cap increase to 90 grants 10 additional talent points distributed across class, hero, and specialization trees, including new Apex Talents as powerful final-node upgrades. The Devourer Demon Hunter spec uses intellect as its primary stat with soul-harvesting mechanics, filling a void-themed mid-range DPS role. Four zones offer distinct environments: reimagined Eversong Woods, the troll stronghold Zul'Aman, the bioluminescent Harandar fungal jungle, and the corrupted Voidstorm. Player housing features fully customizable homes with modular layouts, dyes, and rewards earned from raids and quests, organized into community neighborhoods. The Haranir allied race features extensive customization with bioluminescent body paint in various patterns. Nine raid bosses across three encounters await at launch, alongside eight dungeons, ten delves plus one Nemesis encounter, and the new Slayer's Rise battleground inspired by Alterac Valley mechanics. The Prey system offers hunt-based outdoor progression with cosmetic and mount rewards across three difficulty tiers.
