# Diablo IV: Lord of Hatred Expansion — News Digest

*Date: March 6, 2026*

---

## 1. Lord of Hatred Expansion Announced — Releasing April 28, 2026

Blizzard officially unveiled Diablo IV's second major expansion, Lord of Hatred, at The Game Awards 2025. Set to launch on April 28, 2026, across PC (Battle.net, Steam), PlayStation 4/5, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X|S, the expansion marks the climactic conclusion to Diablo IV's ongoing narrative. Players will face Mephisto, the Lord of Hatred himself, in a final showdown for the fate of Sanctuary. The expansion takes adventurers to the Skovos Isles, a long-requested region that was originally planned for Diablo III before being cut. Skovos holds deep lore significance as the birthplace of humanity on Sanctuary — where Lilith and Inarius first created the human race. The region features volcanic western landscapes, dense eastern forests, and sunken lands between them. At its center stands Temis, the capital city, built with marble edifices and rich textures that reflect the region's connection to both the Blessed Mother and the Heavenly Father. After completing the Lord of Hatred campaign, Temis transforms into a streamlined endgame hub housing all key systems and vendors. The expansion also bundles Vessel of Hatred (including the Spiritborn class) with every pre-purchase, ensuring newcomers have access to all Diablo IV content. Pre-orders are available now across Standard, Deluxe, and Ultimate editions, with instant bonuses including an extra stash tab, two additional character slots, and cosmetic items.

---

## 2. The Warlock — A New Class Commanding the Powers of Hell

The Warlock is one of two new classes arriving with Lord of Hatred on April 28, 2026. Rooted in the forbidden traditions of the Vizjerei mage clan, Warlocks are dark casters who don't serve Hell — they weaponize it. Through relentless discipline, they tear open the veil between worlds, binding demonic forces to protect Sanctuary using the very corruption that threatens it. The class operates on a dual-resource system: Wrath for standard abilities and Dominance for summoning and commanding demons. At level 30, players unlock Soul Shards, the Warlock's defining mechanic. Four archetypes define radically different playstyles: Legion summons hordes of lesser demons to overwhelm enemies; Vanguard lets the player ride a hellhound and transform into a demon spreading fire; Mastermind uses terror and shadows for oppressive crowd control; and Ritualist empowers hellfire rituals for explosive damage. The class introduces several new combat keywords — Hex amplifies Abyss skill damage against weakened targets, Demonform increases maximum life and boosts kill-based damage, Shadowform grants stealth and unhindered movement, and Volatility randomly supercharges Hellfire abilities. Ten Warlock-exclusive Unique items further specialize builds, from the Lurid Pact that grows your Rampage brute to Ae'grom's Schism enabling dual demon summons. Players can begin the class quest "Disciple of the Forbidden" at level 15 through NPC Ked Bardu.

## 3. The Paladin Returns — Available Now via Early Access

The Paladin, one of Diablo's most iconic classes, has returned to the franchise as the seventh playable class in Diablo IV. Unlike the Warlock, which launches with the expansion, the Paladin is available immediately through any Lord of Hatred pre-purchase, making it playable in the base game right now. Lore-wise, Paladins are former outcasts and sinners reborn through the Wardens of Light, an order devoted to defending Sanctuary free from corruption and creed. They wield divine justice to restore hope and strike down evil. Fan-favorite abilities make their comeback, including Auras, Blessed Hammer, Blessed Shield, Condemn, Zeal, and Heaven's Fury. The Paladin introduces a unique class mechanic called the Oath System — a set of sacred paths reflecting codes of faith that Paladins swear to uphold, shaping their abilities and playstyle around different divine doctrines. The Paladin serves as the thematic counterpart to the Warlock: where Warlocks harness Hell's darkness, Paladins channel Heaven's light. Together they bring the total class count to eight when Lord of Hatred launches. The early access strategy mirrors the approach Blizzard used with previous expansions, giving pre-purchasers months of playtime with the new class before launch day. This has proven popular with the community, letting players explore Paladin builds across current seasonal content while building anticipation for the Warlock and the Skovos storyline arriving in late April.

## 4. Massive Skill Tree Rework and Level Cap Increase for All Classes

Lord of Hatred delivers the most significant gameplay overhaul since Diablo IV's 2023 launch, headlined by a complete rewrite of every class's skill tree. Blizzard is removing the old passive-heavy structure that relied on stacking layers of passive bonuses. The new system focuses on modifying how skills actually behave, introducing over 80 additional options per class. Players can now change elements, alter mechanics, and customize abilities in ways previously impossible, dramatically deepening build diversity and theorycrafting potential. Every one of the eight classes — Barbarian, Sorcerer, Rogue, Druid, Necromancer, Spiritborn, Paladin, and Warlock — receives these expanded skill variants, ensuring both new and veteran players have fresh builds to explore. The level cap increases from 60 to 70, though Blizzard has confirmed the time to reach max level remains the same thanks to rebalanced progression curves. Critically, the skill tree rework and level cap increase are free updates — they do not require the Lord of Hatred expansion purchase. This means all Diablo IV players benefit from the enhanced character progression regardless of whether they buy the DLC. A long-awaited native loot filter system also arrives alongside these changes, allowing players to completely hide unwanted item drops from their screen. The loot filter has been one of the community's most persistent requests since launch, and its implementation promises to streamline the endgame grind considerably, reducing inventory management friction and letting players focus on the drops that actually matter for their builds.

---

## 5. Endgame Overhaul — War Plans, Horadric Cube, and Talismans

Lord of Hatred introduces a revitalized endgame built around player choice, mastery, and escalating challenge. The centerpiece is War Plans, a new system that lets players create custom playlists of up to five endgame activities. Drawing from six available modes, players set the activity order and play through them seamlessly in a single chain. As they progress through War Plans, they earn advancement on a unique activity tree for each endgame mode, including the existing Whispers of the Dead system. This gives players unprecedented control over their endgame experience, choosing both what they want to do and what rewards they pursue. The iconic Horadric Cube from Diablo II returns with modernized functionality. Players can transmute a random affix onto any Common, Magic, Rare, or Legendary item, upgrading its rarity in the process — or reverse the procedure to strip unwanted affixes. Most notably, the Cube can upgrade any common item into a Unique of the same type, creating exciting new item chase opportunities and giving previously ignored drops genuine value. A new inventory slot item called the Talisman enables players to equip set bonuses, potentially opening the door for true item sets to return to Diablo IV. Additionally, Echoing Hatred introduces a challenging new mode where players face unyielding threats, and fishing arrives as an entirely new non-combat activity for players seeking a break from demon-slaying. Together, these systems represent the largest endgame expansion Diablo IV has seen, fundamentally reshaping how players engage with content after the campaign.
